So How does it work?

The good thing about this is that the shares are liquid. This means you can access your earnings on monthly bases. As I write this, 1000 shares which are estimated at R1.9 million have just been transferred from the Corporation to us. We will break them down into fractions which we will sell for R100 each, each share you buy will be worth more than the R100, meaning as soon as you own the share, you would already be in profit. This feature protects your capital and maximizes your profits. This system is called leveraging; to control huge amount of a stock with little money. For example: each share costs $145, but it’s been sold to you for R100. Let’s say the market rises by 1% on that day, this will mean on the $145 the interest is 1.45%, which is $1.45. Now if you convert it to South African rand it will be around R20.01, compare this with your initial investment and you have an interest of more than 20%, that is the sweet part of leveraging.
